Festival Bee

Minister of Festivities. Self-appointed. Term: eternal.

Official bee file

Vibe Ceremonial. Booming. Suspiciously prepared.
Natural habitat Parades, podiums, anywhere with bunting or the potential for bunting.
Step style March. Everything is a march. Grocery runs are a march.
Honey order Red, white, and — regrettably — honey only comes in gold. A complaint has been filed.

The lore

Festival Bee appointed himself Minister of Festivities during the very first Honey Festival, and nobody has ever located the paperwork required to un-appoint him. His official position is that every day is at most three business days away from a festival — and if it isn’t, that’s a scheduling failure he intends to raise. At the next festival.

The beard is real. The bow tie is ceremonial. The top hat contains, in descending order: confetti, a backup bow tie, a laminated speech titled “Fellow Bees,” and a smaller hat for emergencies.

During the Honey Festival he personally inspects every celebration for adequate sparkle, buzz, and snack density. His clipboard holds exactly one checkbox. It says “festive?” He has never once checked “no” — because, in his words, “the festival was inside the hive all along. Also, I brought confetti.”

Bee facts

The speech

Has exactly one speech. It has worked at openings, closings, birthdays, and one memorable fire drill.

Fireworks policy

Pro-sparkle, anti-loud. Officially endorses sparklers, bubbles, and dramatic pointing at the sky.

Parade math

Two bees walking in the same direction is legally a parade. He carries spare flags to make it official.

The off-season

There is no off-season. There is only pre-festival.

Favorite Color Hunt color

Firework red

“Festive at any distance.”
